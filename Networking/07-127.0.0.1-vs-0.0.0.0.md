### What is the difference between 0.0.0.0 and 127.0.0.1?

## 127.0.0.1	
- Loopback address (localhost)	
- Used by your computer to talk to itself

1. Refers to your own computer.
2. Used for testing, development, or inter-process communication.
3. Traffic never leaves your machine.

**This calls a server running on your local machine only.**


## 0.0.0.0	
- All IPv4 addresses on the local machine	
- Used by servers to listen on all interfaces

1. Means "listen on all network interfaces".
2. Commonly used in servers to accept traffic from any IP address.
3. It’s not routable — you won’t curl 0.0.0.0, but services use it to bind.
``` python3 -m http.server --bind 0.0.0.0
```

- 27.0.0.1: “I’m talking to myself only.”
- 0.0.0.0: “I’m open to talk to anyone who connects to me.”
